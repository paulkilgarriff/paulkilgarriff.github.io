About Me

Hello! My name is John Smith and I am a final-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at a prestigious university. My research interests are primarily focused on the intersection of artificial intelligence and robotics, with a special emphasis on learning-based control systems for autonomous agents.

Before starting my Ph.D., I completed my Master of Science in Robotics from another top university. I have a passion for building systems that can interact with the physical world in a safe and intelligent manner.

When I'm not in the lab, I enjoy hiking, playing chess, and contributing to open-source projects.

Research

Learning-based Control for Soft Robots

This project explores novel methods for controlling highly deformable soft robots using deep reinforcement learning. We are developing a simulation environment to test our algorithms before deploying them on physical hardware.

Multi-Agent Path Planning

My work on this topic involves creating efficient and collision-free path-planning algorithms for large swarms of robots. Our approach utilizes a decentralized architecture to improve scalability.
