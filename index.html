Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of economics, geography, and data science. I use robust data insights and interdisciplinary approaches to analyze complex, evolving environments. Key areas of interest include the impacts of teleworking on urban structures, spatial analysis of housing and income inequality, and using big data to inform economic policy, especially in the context of financial resilience and climate-related risks.

About Me

I am an economist with an interdisciplinary background in economics and geography, bringing a proven track record in research, policy analysis, and financial resilience. As Principal Investigator, I secured €554k in funding to lead a project on the impacts of teleworking on commuting patterns and housing prices across European cities. In 2022 I was awarded the Barrington Medal by the Statistical and Social Inquiry Society of Ireland for my paper on "Price, Size and Density: Dublin housing in an EU context". My work is driven by a commitment to using robust data insights to inform policy and economic strategy in complex, evolving environments.
